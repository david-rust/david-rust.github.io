Biography

About Feitong

Feitong Lei serves as Senior Biostatistician for the Kentucky Cancer Registry, providing statistical expertise to support population-based cancer surveillance research across the Commonwealth.

Her work encompasses the development and application of advanced statistical methods for analyzing cancer incidence, mortality, and survival data to inform public health policy and research.

Peer-Reviewed Work

2026
Prevalence and Characteristics of Mental Health Disorders Before and After Childhood Cancer Diagnosis
Lei F, McLouth LE, Durbin EB, Tucker TT, Villano JL, Huang B. — Frontiers in Psychology, 16:1680382
Statewide population-based study among Kentucky Medicaid beneficiaries examining the prevalence of mental health disorders in children before and after a cancer diagnosis.
2024
COVID-19 and Rates of Cancer Diagnosis in the US
Burus T, Lei F, Huang B, Christian WJ, Hull PC, Ellis AR, Slavova S, Tucker TC, Lang Kuhs KA. — JAMA Network Open
Investigates how the COVID-19 pandemic affected cancer diagnosis rates across the US, with Lei contributing biostatistical analysis from KCR's Division of Cancer Biostatistics.
2024
Undiagnosed Cancer Cases in the US During the First 10 Months of the COVID-19 Pandemic
Burus T, Lei F, Huang B, Christian WJ, Hull PC, Ellis AR, Slavova S, Tucker TC, Lang Kuhs KA. — JAMA Oncology
Estimates the number of cancer cases that went undiagnosed during the first 10 months of the COVID-19 pandemic due to disruptions in screening and care-seeking behavior.
2024
Geographic Variation of HPV-Associated Cancer Incidence in Kentucky Using Spatial Scan Statistics
Trott S, Lei F, Christian WJ, Mao X, Lehmkuhl B, Kejner A. — Cancer Causes and Control
Applies spatial scan statistics to Kentucky Cancer Registry data to identify geographic clusters of HPV-associated cancer incidence across the Commonwealth.
2023
Influence of Depression on Breast Cancer Treatment and Survival: A Kentucky Population-Based Study
Lei F, Vanderpool RC, McLouth LE, Romond EH, Chen Q, Durbin EB, Tucker TC, Tai E, Huang B. — Cancer, 129(12):1821–1835
First-authored study examining how depression before or after a breast cancer diagnosis affects treatment receipt and survival outcomes in 6,054 Kentucky patients.
