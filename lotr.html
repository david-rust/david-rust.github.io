✦ The Founding of the Great Registry ✦

Hail, Wanderer,
and Welcome to the Registry

In the nine hundred and ninetieth year since the founding of the Republic, the General Assembly of the Commonwealth did pass into law the great ordinance formally establishing the Kentucky Cancer Registry — henceforth to serve as the keeper of all records pertaining to the dread affliction of cancer, across all one hundred and twenty counties of the realm.

The Registry is bound in fellowship with the National Cancer Institute's own Surveillance, Epidemiology, and End Results programme, and likewise with the CDC's National Programme of Cancer Registries. As an honoured member of the NAACCR, the KCR renders faithful service to healers, scholars, stewards of public health, and the great councils of policy — ensuring that no county of the Commonwealth shall be left uncharted in the great reckoning.

✦ From the Loremaster's Lips ✦

The Loremaster Speaks

Eric Durbin Loremaster of Cancer Informatics
✦ Transcribed and rendered into the common tongue from a proclamation of the Year 2016

Registries of affliction are the unseen pillars upon which the great work of healing is raised. Without faithful, population-based records, no healer, no scholar, no steward of public health may rightly know where the sickness is gathered most thickly — nor allocate their remedies wisely, nor measure whether their preventive labours have borne any fruit. The Kentucky Cancer Registry exists to be the vigilant eye of that great public effort, watching over all one hundred and twenty fiefs of the Commonwealth.

Since the Registry entered into the fellowship of the NCI's SEER programme in the year 2000 — one of a mere seventeen such orders across the whole of the nation — we have pressed ever onward beyond what any had thought a registry capable of. By the arts of Natural Language Processing, we now receive the tidings of the healers — their pathology reports — in near-real time from every corner of the realm. We learn of a cancer's arrival almost as swiftly as the treating physician himself. And this swiftness is no mere vanity: in late-stage affliction of the lung, the patient is granted but nine months upon middle-earth. To reach them soon enough to offer enrolment in a healing trial may mean the difference between shadow and light.

And beyond the welfare of individual souls, the Registry serves as a great virtual storehouse — a tissue repository in all but name — tracing the whereabouts of samples taken at surgery or biopsy across the length of the realm. Because this record-keeping encompasses the full population, the wisdom drawn from any single enquiry may be extended to the whole of Kentucky, raising the quality of the science immeasurably. That is what separates a true registry from a mere ledger of names: the power to bind the data back to the living people and the communities from which it sprang.

The original proclamation may be read in full at the Halls of the UK College of Medicine →
